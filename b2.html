PART I: ADVANCED DISCOURSE

UNIT 1: LE SUBJONCTIF PASSÉ (PAST SUBJUNCTIVE)

1.1 Introduction & Formation

At the B2 level, expressing emotion or doubt about an event that occurred in the past requires the subjonctif passé.

Formation: Subjonctif présent of être or avoir + past participle.

  • Avoir: que j'aie parlé, que tu aies fini, qu'il ait vendu.
  • Être: que je sois allé(e), qu'elle se soit lavée, que nous soyons partis.

1.2 Usage and Concordance

Use the past subjunctive when the main clause (trigger) is in the present, but the dependent clause refers to a completed past action.

  • Je suis déçu qu'ils n'aient pas gagné le match hier. (I am disappointed that they did not win the game yesterday.)
  • Il est dommage que tu sois tombé malade pendant tes vacances. (It is a shame that you fell sick during your vacation.)
  • Bien qu'elle ait beaucoup étudié, elle a échoué à l'examen. (Although she studied a lot, she failed the exam.)

1.3 Practice Examples

  • Je doute qu'il ait lu ce rapport avant la réunion. (I doubt he read this report before the meeting.)
  • Le professeur exige que le devoir ait été soumis avant minuit. (The professor demands that the assignment have been submitted before midnight. - Note the passive past subjunctive here!)
  • C'est le meilleur film québécois que j'aie jamais vu. (It is the best Québécois film I have ever seen.) - Superlatives often trigger the subjunctive.

🇫🇷 Real-Life Scene – Discussing a Concluded Project:

Deux cadres analysent les résultats décevants du dernier trimestre.

"Je suis vraiment contrarié que notre campagne marketing n'ait pas généré les ventes escomptées."
"Moi aussi. Je suis étonné que les consommateurs n'aient pas réagi à la promotion."
"Il est possible que nous ayons mal ciblé notre clientèle. Bien que l'équipe ait travaillé sans relâche, les résultats ne sont pas là."
"Je propose qu'on révise la stratégie. Il faut absolument que nous ayons corrigé le tir avant le mois prochain."

(Two executives analyze the disappointing results of the last quarter. "I am really upset that our marketing campaign did not generate the anticipated sales." "Me too. I am surprised that consumers did not react to the promotion." "It is possible that we wrongly targeted our clientele. Although the team worked relentlessly, the results are not there." "I propose we revise the strategy. We absolutely must have corrected our course before next month.")

1.4 Canadian French Notes (🍁)

Canadian French Note (🍁):

  • In everyday spoken Quebec French, the subjonctif passé is often replaced by a simpler construction, such as using the indicative: "Je suis fâché que t'as pas fait la vaisselle." (Instead of the correct: "que tu n'aies pas fait"). However, at the B2 level, particularly in writing or formal situations, mastering the proper subjunctive is crucial.
  • 🚨 Exceptions & Edge Cases: The subjonctif passé is essential in B2 for expressing past emotions/doubts about a completed event: "Je suis ravi qu'il soit venu." It uses the present subjunctive of the auxiliary + past participle.
  • ❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid: Don't use the subjunctive after après que (after). It takes the indicative! Use it only after avant que (before).
  • 📖 Extended Vocabulary Expansion:
    • Il est douteux que - It is doubtful that
    • Nier que - To deny that
    • Craindre que - To fear that
    • Bien que - Even though
  • 🗣️ Rapid-Fire Practice:
    • Je suis surpris qu'il ait dit ça. -> I am surprised he said that.
    • Il faut que nous soyons partis avant midi. -> We must have left before noon.
    • Je regrette qu'elle n'ait pas pu venir. -> I regret that she couldn't come.
    • Je doute qu'ils aient fini. -> I doubt they have finished.
    • C'est dommage que tu aies raté ça. -> It's a shame you missed that.

UNIT 2: THE PASSIVE VOICE IN DEPTH

2.1 Advanced Passive Structures

In B2, you will encounter the passive voice across all tenses, particularly in bureaucratic texts and journalism.
Formation: Conjugate être in the desired tense + past participle (agreed with the subject).

  • Imparfait: L'immeuble était rénové par la ville. (The building was being renovated by the city.)
  • Futur: La décision sera prise la semaine prochaine. (The decision will be made next week.)
  • Conditionnel: Des mesures plus strictes seraient adoptées. (Stricter measures would be adopted.)
  • Passé Composé: Le suspect a été arrêté. (The suspect was arrested.)

2.2 Avoiding the Passive (On, Se)

French strongly prefers the active voice. Instead of the passive, B2 speakers often use "On" or reflexive verbs (la voix pronominale).

  • Passive: Cette porte est fermée à 18h.
  • With "On": On ferme cette porte à 18h. (They close this door at 6pm.)
  • With "Se": Cette porte se ferme à 18h. (This door closes [itself] at 6pm.)

Another example:

  • Passive: Le français est parlé au Québec.
  • Reflexive: Le français se parle au Québec.

2.3 Use in Media and Formal Texts

In news, the passive emphasizes the action rather than the doer:

  • Une entente a été conclue entre le gouvernement et le syndicat tard hier soir. Les détails seront dévoilés demain matin. (An agreement was reached between the government and the union late last night. The details will be revealed tomorrow morning.)

🇫🇷 Real-Life Scene – Listening to the Traffic Report:

Vous écoutez le bulletin de circulation à ICI Première.

"Et maintenant, la circulation: L'autoroute 40 en direction est a été complètement bloquée à la hauteur du boulevard Saint-Michel à cause d'un carambolage. Plusieurs véhicules sont impliqués. Il est conseillé de prendre l'avenue Papineau, bien que celle-ci soit également congestionnée."

(You are listening to the traffic report on ICI Première. "And now, traffic: Highway 40 Eastbound has been completely blocked near Saint-Michel Boulevard because of a pile-up. Several vehicles are involved. It is advised to take Papineau Avenue, although it is also congested.")

2.4 Canadian French Notes (🍁)

Canadian French Note (🍁):

  • In Quebec journalism, the passive voice is as common as in France. However, pay attention to specific administrative vocabulary. For example, a contract might be "octroyé" (granted/awarded) rather than just "donné".
  • "À la hauteur de" is a very common spatial expression in Quebec meaning "at the level of" or "near" (often used when giving directions on a highway).
  • 🚨 Exceptions & Edge Cases: The conditionnel passé expresses regrets or missed opportunities ("I would have..."). It requires the conditional of the auxiliary (aurais/serais) + past participle. Note the agreement with être verbs.
  • ❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid: In English we say "He should have gone." In French, this is the conditional past of devoir: Il aurait dû aller, not "Il devrait avoir allé."
  • 📖 Extended Vocabulary Expansion:
    • Un regret - A regret
    • Un reproche - A reproach / criticism
    • Il aurait mieux valu - It would have been better to
    • Rater une occasion - To miss an opportunity
  • 🗣️ Rapid-Fire Practice:
    • J'aurais aimé te voir. -> I would have liked to see you.
    • Tu aurais pu me le dire ! -> You could have told me!
    • Nous y serions allés si... -> We would have gone there if...
    • Il n'aurait pas dû faire ça. -> He shouldn't have done that.
    • Qu'aurais-tu fait à ma place ? -> What would you have done in my place?

UNIT 3: COMPLEX DISCOURSE MARKERS

3.1 Structuring a Nuanced Argument

To pass a B2 exam (like the DELF or TEFAQ), you must string ideas together logically.

  • Pour commencer: En premier lieu, tout d'abord, à première vue.
  • Pour ajouter une idée: De plus, par ailleurs, en outre.
  • Pour illustrer: À titre d'exemple, notamment, en d'autres termes.
  • Pour conclure: En définitive, tout bien considéré, en somme.

3.2 Concession & Opposition

Concession allows you to acknowledge a counter-argument before refuting it, a hallmark of advanced speech.

  • Bien que / Quoique (+ subjonctif): Although. Bien qu'il soit cher, ce quartier est idéal.
  • Cependant / Néanmoins / Toutefois: However / Nevertheless. Il pleuvait; néanmoins, nous sommes sortis.
  • En revanche / Par contre: On the other hand.
  • Malgré (+ nom): Despite. Malgré la grève, les cours ont eu lieu.
  • Certes..., mais...: Admittedly..., but... Certes, l'intelligence artificielle présente des risques, mais elle offre aussi des opportunités immenses.

3.3 Emphasizing a Point

  • Il va de soi que... (It goes without saying that...)
  • Il n'en reste pas moins que... (The fact remains that...)
  • Quoi qu'il en soit, (Be that as it may, / Anyway,)

🇫🇷 Real-Life Scene – Defending an Urban Planning Choice:

Lors d'une assemblée citoyenne, un élu défend un nouveau parc.

"Certes, ce projet de réaménagement coûtera cher aux contribuables. Cependant, il faut voir cela comme un investissement à long terme. En effet, l'ajout d'espaces verts réduira les îlots de chaleur. Bien que certains commerçants craignent une perte de stationnement, il n'en reste pas moins que la majorité des résidents réclament un milieu de vie plus sain. En définitive, l'amélioration de la qualité de vie compensera largement les inconvénients initiaux."

(During a town hall meeting, an elected official defends a new park. "Admittedly, this redevelopment project will be expensive for taxpayers. However, we must see this as a long-term investment. Indeed, the addition of green spaces will reduce heat islands. Although some merchants fear a loss of parking, the fact remains that the majority of residents are demanding a healthier living environment. Ultimately, the improvement in quality of life will largely compensate for the initial inconveniences.")

3.4 Canadian French Notes (🍁)

Canadian French Note (🍁):

  • "Dans le fond" is widely used in spoken Quebec French as a filler or a concluding discourse marker, meaning "basically," "essentially," or "in the end."
  • "Par contre" is used much more frequently than "en revanche" in Quebec.
  • 🚨 Exceptions & Edge Cases: The passive voice is formed with être + past participle. But French prefers the active voice or "on". Instead of saying "La maison a été vendue," it sounds more natural to say On a vendu la maison in casual speech.
  • ❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid: Don't forget that in the passive voice, the past participle ALWAYS agrees with the subject. "La lettre a été écrite par Marie."
  • 📖 Extended Vocabulary Expansion:
    • Subir - To subject / undergo
    • Être censé - To be supposed to
    • Être accusé de - To be accused of
    • Par le biais de - By means of
  • 🗣️ Rapid-Fire Practice:
    • Le voleur a été arrêté. -> The thief was arrested.
    • Cette chanson est chantée par Céline Dion. -> This song is sung by Céline Dion.
    • Le projet sera complété demain. -> The project will be completed tomorrow.
    • On m'a offert un emploi. -> I was offered a job (They offered me a job).
    • La décision a été prise. -> The decision has been made.

UNIT 4: EXPRESSING NUANCED OPINIONS

4.1 Beyond Good and Bad

At B2, you must evaluate situations with nuance rather than simple approval or disapproval.

  • Je suis partagé(e) sur la question. (I have mixed feelings on the issue.)
  • Cette mesure soulève plusieurs interrogations essentielles. (This measure raises several essential questions.)
  • D'un côté..., mais de l'autre... (On one hand..., but on the other...)
  • Il faut nuancer ce propos. (We must add nuance to this statement.)
  • Ce n'est ni tout blanc ni tout noir. (It's not black and white.)

4.2 Expressing Certainty & Doubt

Certainty (Indicatif):

  • Il est indéniable que (It is undeniable that)
  • Il est évident que (It is obvious that)
  • Je suis persuadé(e) que (I am convinced that)

Doubt (Subjonctif):

  • Il est peu probable que (It is unlikely that)
  • Je ne suis pas certain(e) que (I am not sure that)
  • Il se peut que (It is possible that)

4.3 Participating in Debates

  • Interrupting politely: Si je peux me permettre, je voudrais ajouter que... (If I may, I would like to add that...)
  • Asking for clarification: Pouvez-vous préciser votre pensée? (Can you clarify your thought?)
  • Refuting an argument: Je comprends l'idée, mais force est de constater que la réalité est bien différente. (I understand the idea, but it must be noted that reality is quite different.)

🇫🇷 Real-Life Scene – A Radio Talk Show (Ligne Ouverte):

Un auditeur appelle à la radio pour débattre d'une loi sur la langue.

"Bonjour. Écoutez, je suis assez partagé sur ce nouveau projet de loi. D'un côté, il est indéniable qu'il faut protéger la langue française au Québec. C'est notre identité. Mais de l'autre côté, je ne suis pas certain que contraindre les petites entreprises soit la bonne approche."
"Vous pensez donc que le gouvernement va trop loin?" (Animateur)
"Il faut nuancer. Le gouvernement a raison d'agir, mais il se pourrait que cette loi nuise à l'attraction de talents internationaux. Quoi qu'il en soit, nous avons besoin d'un vrai débat de société sur la question et non de lois précipitées."

(A listener calls the radio to debate a language law. "Hello. Listen, I have mixed feelings about this new bill. On one hand, it is undeniable that we must protect the French language in Quebec. It is our identity. But on the other hand, I am not certain that constraining small businesses is the right approach." "So you think the government is going too far?" (Host) "We must nuance this. The government is right to act, but it could be that this law harms the attraction of international talent. Be that as it may, we need a real societal debate on the issue and not rushed laws.")

4.4 Canadian French Notes (🍁)

Canadian French Note (🍁):

  • Talk radio, or "les radios d'opinion" (sometimes critically called "les radios poubelles" if they are sensationalist), is a massive part of Quebec culture, especially in Quebec City.
  • Debating politics and language rights is a favorite pastime in Quebec society. Being able to formulate balanced, nuanced arguments (using "d'un côté," "de l'autre") is the key to participating in these passionate discussions effectively.
  • 🚨 Exceptions & Edge Cases: The gérondif (en + -ant) implies two actions happening simultaneously by the SAME subject. "Je mange en regardant la télé." If subjects differ, you must use a relative clause.
  • ❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid: Don't use the present participle as an adjective without checking agreement. As a verb (gérondif) it never agrees. As an adjective ("une personne charmante"), it does agree.
  • 📖 Extended Vocabulary Expansion:
    • Tout en - While (emphasizes simultaneity or contrast)
    • Provoquant - Provoking / Shocking
    • Fascinant(e) - Fascinating
    • Épuisant(e) - Exhausting
  • 🗣️ Rapid-Fire Practice:
    • Il est parti en pleurant. -> He left crying.
    • J'ai appris le français en écoutant des podcasts. -> I learned French by listening to podcasts.
    • C'est un travail fatigant. -> It's a tiring job.
    • Tout en souriant, elle a refusé. -> While smiling, she refused.
    • En cherchant mes clés, j'ai trouvé 10 dollars. -> While looking for my keys, I found $10.

5.2 Formal Emails (Les courriels professionnels)

B2 requires you to write formal, nuanced emails without sounding robotic.

  • Opening: Bonjour Madame/Monsieur [Nom], Suite à notre conversation téléphonique... (Following our telephone conversation...)
  • Making a request: Je vous serais reconnaissant(e) de bien vouloir m'envoyer... (I would be grateful if you would send me...)
  • Attaching a file: Veuillez trouver ci-joint le dossier en question. (Please find attached the file in question.)
  • Following up: Je me permets de vous relancer concernant... (Allow me to follow up with you regarding...)
  • Closing: Cordialement, (Regards,) ou Salutations distinguées, (Sincerely, - formal).

5.3 Running a Meeting (Animer une réunion)

  • Opening: Merci à tous d'être présents. L'objectif de la réunion d'aujourd'hui est de... (Thank you all for being here. The objective of today's meeting is to...)
  • Transitioning: Passons maintenant au deuxième point de l'ordre du jour. (Let's move now to the second item on the agenda.)
  • Dealing with interruptions: Si vous permettez, j'aimerais terminer mon explication avant de prendre vos questions. (If you'll allow it, I would like to finish my explanation before taking your questions.)
  • Concluding: Pour résumer, nous avons convenu que... (To summarize, we have agreed that...)

🇫🇷 Real-Life Scene – Discussing Quarterly Results:

Une réunion de gestion dans une grande entreprise.

"Bienvenue à tous. Tel qu'indiqué à l'ordre du jour, nous allons examiner les résultats du troisième trimestre. Comme vous pouvez le constater dans le rapport ci-joint, notre chiffre d'affaires a augmenté de 5%."
"C'est une excellente nouvelle. Cependant, la rentabilité de notre filiale ontarienne a chuté. Quelles en sont les causes?"
"Il semblerait que les coûts d'exploitation aient considérablement augmenté cette année. Le conseil d'administration exige que nous présentions un plan de restructuration d'ici vendredi."
"Je vais charger l'équipe des finances de préparer une analyse détaillée. Nous ferons un suivi lors de la prochaine réunion."

(A management meeting in a large company. "Welcome everyone. As indicated on the agenda, we are going to examine the third quarter results. As you can observe in the attached report, our revenue has increased by 5%." "This is excellent news. However, the profitability of our Ontario subsidiary has dropped. What are the causes of this?" "It would seem that operating costs have increased considerably this year. The board of directors demands that we present a restructuring plan by Friday." "I am going to task the finance team with preparing a detailed analysis. We will follow up at the next meeting.")

5.4 Canadian French Notes (🍁)

Canadian French Note (🍁):

  • In Quebec, the word "courriel" (a blend of courrier + électronique) is universally used instead of the English "e-mail/mail" used in France.
  • A meeting is universally referred to as "un meeting" or "une rencontre" in spoken Quebec French, though "une réunion" remains standard in writing.
  • Le PDG (Président-directeur général) is the equivalent of the CEO.
  • 🚨 Exceptions & Edge Cases: In B2, you must structure arguments fluidly using connectors. Words like d'une part / d'autre part (on the one hand / on the other hand) organize multi-point debates perfectly.
  • ❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid: Don't translate "I disagree" as "Je suis désagréable". It's Je ne suis pas d'accord. "Désagréable" means unpleasant/rude.
  • 📖 Extended Vocabulary Expansion:
    • Un atout - An asset / advantage
    • Un inconvénient - A disadvantage
    • Souligner - To emphasize / point out
    • Être convaincu(e) - To be convinced
  • 🗣️ Rapid-Fire Practice:
    • Je tiens à souligner que... -> I want to point out that...
    • L'avantage principal est... -> The main advantage is...
    • Il faut peser le pour et le contre. -> We must weigh the pros and cons.
    • C'est un argument de poids. -> It's a strong argument.
    • Je suis tout à fait contre cette idée. -> I am completely against this idea.

🇫🇷 Real-Life Scene – Discussing a Tenancy Dispute:

Un locataire consulte un avocat au sujet d'une éviction.

"Maître, mon propriétaire m'a envoyé un avis d'éviction la semaine dernière. Il prétend vouloir loger sa fille dans mon appartement."
"Je comprends. Au Québec, la reprise de logement est encadrée de façon très stricte par le Tribunal administratif du logement. Avez-vous signé le document?"
"Non, j'ai refusé de le signer car je doute de ses intentions. Je crois qu'il veut simplement rénover et augmenter le loyer."

8.2 Writing Essays (Dissertations)

The French educational system strongly values rhetorical structure. A classic essay format is:

  1. Introduction: Amener le sujet (hook), poser la problématique (the central question), annoncer le plan (outline the structure).
  2. Thèse: Argument in favor.
  3. Antithèse: Counter-argument (nuance).
  4. Synthèse: Resolving the paradox or offering a compromise.
  5. Conclusion: Résumé (summary) AND ouverture (opening to a broader topic).

Note: This rigid "thèse-antithèse-synthèse" structure is less mandatory in Quebec than in France, but knowing it is essential for B2/C1 exams.

UNIT 12: QUEBEC IDENTITY

12.1 Language Laws (La Charte de la langue française)

You cannot understand Quebec without understanding La Loi 101 (Bill 101), passed in 1977.

  • It made French the sole official language of the state, courts, and workplaces.
  • It requires all commercial signage to be predominantly in French (l'affichage commercial).
  • It mandates that children of immigrants attend French-language schools (les enfants d'allophones doivent fréquenter l'école francophone) until the end of high school. Children whose parents studied in English in Canada can attend English schools.

Vocabulary:

  • Un francophone: whose mother tongue is French.
  • Un anglophone: whose mother tongue is English.
  • Un allophone: whose mother tongue is neither French nor English.

12.2 The Sovereignist Movement

The debate over whether Quebec should separate from Canada and become an independent country has dominated politics for decades.

  • Les souverainistes / indépendantistes: Believe Quebec should be a sovereign nation to protect its language, culture, and economic interests.
  • Les fédéralistes: Believe Quebec should remain a province within the Canadian federation.
  • Les référendums: 1980 (60% No) and 1995 (50.58% No). The debate has since cooled, but the structural tension remains.

12.3 Cultural Protectionism

Quebec acts effectively like a nation-state within a state to protect its culture from American and English-Canadian influence. The concept of "l'exception culturelle" is vital: the idea that cultural goods (music, TV, books) should not be treated merely as commercial products but must be subsidized and protected by quotas.

UNIT 13: HUMOR & SARCASM

13.1 Humor in Quebec

Stand-up comedy is arguably the most popular art form in Quebec. To understand humor in a second language is proof of C1 fluency, but at B2, you should start recognizing the structures.

  • L'autodérision (Self-deprecation): Quebec humor relies heavily on making fun of oneself, rather than mocking the audience aggressively.
  • L'absurde (Absurdity): Situational comedy that escalates to ridiculous extremes.

13.2 Irony and Sarcasm

Francophones frequently use sarcasm, often without changing their tone of voice. Context is key.

  • C'est ça, oui. (Yeah, right.)
  • Tu parles d'une bonne idée! (What a great idea! → Meaning: What a terrible idea).
  • On est bien barrés. (We're in a great spot. → Meaning: We're doomed / stuck).
  • C'est le fun en maudit! (That's so fun! → If said with rolling eyes while shoveling snow, it means the opposite).

13.3 Expressing Irony in Debate

  • C'est la meilleure! (That's the best one yet! / That takes the cake!)
  • Et on s'étonne que... (And we wonder why...) Et on s'étonne que les jeunes ne veuillent plus travailler dans la restauration!

UNIT 15: FORMAL WRITING (L'ÉCRIT)

15.1 La Synthèse (Synthesis)

A classic B2/C1 academic exercise. You receive 3 documents on a topic and must write a cohesive essay summarizing the debate without giving your own opinion.

  • Introduction: Les trois documents étudiés traitent du thème de... Le premier, un article du Devoir, souligne que... Le second... Enfin, le troisième...
  • Phrases clés: À la lecture de ces textes, il ressort que... / Les auteurs s'accordent pour dire que... / En revanche, le document C remet en question la théorie de l'auteur du document A.

15.2 Le Compte-Rendu (Report / Minutes)

Writing a formal report or meeting minutes requires the use of reported speech and formal vocabulary.

  • Opening: La séance a été ouverte à 9h00.
  • Body: M. Lavoie a exposé la situation financière. Ensuite, Mme Côté a proposé de... Une discussion s'en est suivie concernant...
  • Closing: Le comité a statué que... La séance a été levée à 11h30.

15.3 Complex Sentence Structure

To achieve a high B2 score in writing, use relative pronouns (qui, que, dont, où) and participial phrases (Le gérant ayant refusé notre offre, nous...) to combine simple sentences.

  • Simple: J'ai acheté un livre. L'auteur a gagné un prix. Je vais lire son livre ce soir.
  • Complex B2: J'ai acheté le roman de cet auteur dont on parle beaucoup et qui a récemment gagné un prix, roman que je compte dévorer dès ce soir.

UNIT 16: PRONUNCIATION & REGISTER (PHONÉTIQUE)

16.1 Liaisons (Obligatory vs. Optional)

At B2, you should know when a liaison is required, forbidden, or optional (formal register).

  • Obligatory: After pronouns (Nous_avons), after determiners (les_amis), after short adjectives (un petit_arbre).
  • Forbidden: After "et" (un chat et / un chien), before aspirated H (les / homards), after a singular noun (un chat / intelligent).
  • Optional (Marks high register): After plural nouns (des amis_intimes), after verbs (il faut_y aller), after polysyllabic adverbs (cependant_il est venu).

16.2 Quebec Vowel Diphthongization

Quebec French is characterized by the lengthening and diphthongizing of long vowels, especially on stressed syllables (at the end of rhythmic groups). Understanding this is key to B2 listening comprehension.

  • Père: pronounced roughly like /paɛ̯ʁ/ (sounding almost like 'pair' but stretched).
  • Fête: pronounced /faɛ̯t/ (almost like 'fight' in some regions).
  • Chose: pronounced /ʃoʊ̯z/ (like the English 'shows').

16.3 Affrication (Ts and Dz)

The single most defining feature of the Quebec accent is the affrication of T and D before "i" and "u" sounds.

  • T becomes TS: Petit is pronounced "ptsi". Tu is pronounced "tsu". Parti is "partsi."
  • D becomes DZ: Dieu is pronounced "Dzieu." Du is pronounced "Dzu." Mardi is "Mardzi."

PART V: REVIEW & B2 EXAM PREP

UNIT 17: GRAMMAR CAPSTONE

17.1 The Subjunctive vs. Indicative Check

In B2, you are expected to switch effortlessly between moods based on the trigger phrase.

  • Indicatif (Certainty): Je pense que, je crois que, j'espère que, il est clair que.
    • Je pense qu'il vient ce soir. (I think he is coming tonight.)
  • Subjonctif (Doubt/Emotion/Necessity): Je ne pense pas que, il faut que, je suis content que, bien que.
    • Je ne pense pas qu'il vienne ce soir. (I don't think he is coming tonight.)

17.2 Preposition Pitfalls

  • À vs. De: Continuer à faire qch, vs. Essayer de faire qch.
    • J'ai réussi à le convaincre de venir. (I succeeded in convincing him to come.)
  • Pour vs. Pendant: Use pour for planned future duration; use pendant for completed past duration.
    • Je pars au Québec pour deux abs. (I am leaving for Quebec for two years.)
    • J'ai habité à Montréal pendant cinq ans. (I lived in Montreal for five years.)

17.3 Advanced Pronouns (Y / En placement)

  • Y replaces à + noun (ideas/places). Tu penses à ton avenir? Oui, j'y pense.
  • En replaces de + noun (quantities/ideas). Tu as besoin de ce dossier? Oui, j'en ai besoin.
  • Order: Subject + (me/te/se/nous/vous) + (le/la/les) + (lui/leur) + (y) + (en) + Verb.
    • Il y a des pommes? Donne-m'en deux. (Are there apples? Give me two of them.)

UNIT 19: EXAM STRATEGIES (DELF/TEFAQ)

19.1 Listening Comprehension (TEFAQ)

If you are applying for Quebec immigration, the TEFAQ (Test d'évaluation de français pour l'accès au Québec) is your goal. A B2 score earns you maximum points.

  • Strategy: Read the questions before the audio starts to anticipate the topic.
  • Trap: The audio will often mention numbers or names that are in the wrong answers just to trick you. Listen for synonyms, not exact matches.

19.2 Oral Expression

The DELF B2 involves a 20-minute monologue and debate based on an article.

  • Strategy 1: Plan your structure. Introduction (hook, source, thesis), Body (2-3 arguments clearly separated by discourse markers), Conclusion.
  • Strategy 2: Self-correction. If you make a grammar mistake, correct yourself immediately! (e.g., "Il faut que je fais... pardon, il faut que je fasse"). Examiners reward self-awareness.
  • Strategy 3: Engage the examiner. When they challenge you, use concession ("Je comprends votre point de vue, cependant...").

19.3 Written Comprehension & Expression

  • Expression: You will usually write a formal letter (e.g., to a mayor to complain about a city project). Use formal greetings (Veuillez agréer...), structure your paragraphs, and use the conditional for politeness (Je souhaiterais vous faire part de...).

19.4 Canadian French Notes (🍁)

Canadian French Note (🍁):

  • The TEFAQ audio specifically features accents from Quebec and France. You must be comfortable hearing both. Do not rely solely on Parisian audio materials to prepare.
  • 🚨 Exceptions & Edge Cases: Meals differ entirely. In France: Petit-déjeuner (morning), Déjeuner (noon), Dîner (night). In Quebec: Déjeuner (morning), Dîner (noon), Souper (night).
  • ❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid: Using Glymmer anglicisms in France vs Quebec. In France, they say "le weekend", "le parking". Quebec aggressively defends French and uses la fin de semaine, le stationnement.
  • 📖 Extended Vocabulary Expansion:
    • Un chandail - A sweater (QC) vs Un pull (FR)
    • Des espadrilles - Sneakers (QC) vs Des baskets (FR)
    • Un cellulaire - Cellphone (QC) vs Un portable (FR)
    • Un breuvage - A beverage/drink (QC) vs Une boisson (FR)
  • 🗣️ Rapid-Fire Practice:
    • On va magasiner en fin de semaine ? (QC) -> Are we going shopping this weekend?

    • Voulez-vous un breuvage ? (QC) -> Would you like a beverage?

20.3 Next Steps: Towards C1

What separates a B2 from a C1 (Advanced)?

  • B2: You can communicate clearly, argue effectively, and understand most of the news. You make occasional errors but can self-correct.
  • C1: You have fluid, spontaneous expression. You understand cultural references, humor, idioms, and fast-paced slang seamlessly. You can read complex literary or academic texts without a dictionary.
